R.A. 9344 - Juvenile Delinquency

Republic Act 9344, also known as the "Juvenile Justice and Welfare Act of 2006," is a law in the Philippines aimed at promoting the rights and well-being of children in conflict with the law.

Key Provisions:

  • Diversion of Juvenile Cases
  • Protective Custody
  • Community-based Programs
  • Child Welfare Programs

Objectives:

  1. Ensure the rights of the child are protected throughout the legal process.
  2. Promote rehabilitation and reintegration rather than punishment.
  3. Establish a comprehensive juvenile justice and welfare system.

Implementation:

The law outlines procedures for handling cases involving children in conflict with the law, emphasizing restorative justice and rehabilitation over punitive measures.
